Technical Documentation

Actuarial methodology, assumptions, and reliances.

Version 14.0
1. Mathematical Methodology

This application utilizes a discrete-time stochastic Monte Carlo simulation engine. Asset paths are projected using Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) principles, adapted to support non-normal distributions (fat tails).

1.1 Asset Path Generation

For each asset class $i$, the simulated monthly return $R_{i,t}$ is calculated as:

$$ R_{i,t} = \left( (1 + \mu_i)^{1/12} - 1 \right) + \frac{\sigma_i}{\sqrt{12}} Z_{i,t}^{cf} $$

Where $\mu_i$ is the arithmetic mean annual return, $\sigma_i$ is the annual volatility, and $Z_{i,t}^{cf}$ is the correlated, kurtosis-adjusted standard normal variable.

1.2 Correlation Structure

To model dependencies, random variables are correlated using a two-factor (Equity/Credit) loading model derived from Cholesky decomposition. For independent standard normal variables $z_1, z_2, z_3 \sim N(0,1)$:

$$ Z_{i,t}^{gauss} = c_{eq} z_1 + c_{cr} z_2 + \sqrt{\max(0, 1 - c_{eq}^2 - c_{cr}^2)} z_3 $$
1.3 Fat-Tail Adjustment (Cornish-Fisher)

Standard models systematically underestimate market crashes. To correct this, the Gaussian variable $Z_{i,t}^{gauss}$ is transformed using the Cornish-Fisher Expansion based on the specified Excess Kurtosis $K_i$:

$$ Z_{cf\_raw} = Z_{gauss} + \frac{K_i}{24} \left( Z_{gauss}^3 - 3 Z_{gauss} \right) $$

To ensure this transformation does not inadvertently inflate the target volatility, the variable is re-standardized by its analytical drift variance factor $\sqrt{1 + K_i^2/96}$:

$$ Z_{i,t}^{cf} = \frac{Z_{cf\_raw}}{\sqrt{1 + \frac{K_i^2}{96}}} $$
2. Example Calculation

To illustrate the accumulation and discounting logic, assume a single contribution of £10,000 invested for exactly 12 months in a hypothetical asset.

  • Core Inflation ($i$): $2.5\%$ per annum.
  • Realized Nominal Return ($r_{nom}$): $6.5\%$ for the 12-month period.
Step 1: Nominal Growth

The portfolio simply grows at the simulated nominal rate:

$$ \text{Pot}_{nom} = £10,000 \times (1 + 0.065) = £10,650 $$
Step 2: Real Terms Discounting

To convert the future sum into 'today's money' (purchasing power), it is deflated by cumulative core inflation over time $T$ (where $T=1$ year):

$$ \text{Pot}_{real} = \frac{£10,650}{(1 + 0.025)^1} \approx £10,390.24 $$

Note on Contributions: Salary-based contributions are modeled to grow nominally at $(1 + \text{Core Inflation}) \times (1 + \text{Real Salary Growth}) - 1$ to preserve wage purchasing power over the accumulation phase.

3. Reliances & Limitations

Users of this model must be aware of its boundaries. Projections generated do not constitute formal financial advice.

  • Parameter Risk: Capital Market Assumptions (CMAs) are point-in-time, forward-looking estimates. Historical correlations and tail risks may break down in future macroeconomic regimes.
  • Frictionless Environment: Output values are broadly stated Gross of Fees. They do not account for platform administration charges, underlying fund OCFs, or transaction costs (bid/offer spreads) unless explicitly modeled by the user via negative return adjustments.
  • Taxation Assumptions: The model projects a gross capital pot and assumes a tax-advantaged wrapper (e.g., Pension or ISA). It makes no allowances for Income Tax on withdrawal, Capital Gains Tax, or Lifetime Allowance (LTA) surcharges.
  • Sequence of Returns Risk: While the model correctly penalizes geometric compounding via volatility drag during the accumulation phase, it is not currently calibrated to assess pound-cost ravaging during the decumulation/drawdown phase.
  • Behavioral Independence: The simulation assumes strict adherence to the glidepath and continuous rebalancing. Real-world behavioral risks, such as panic selling during drawdowns or pausing contributions, are excluded.
